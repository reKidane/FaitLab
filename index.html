The Fait Lab at Ben-Gurion University of the Negev investigates the metabolic networks that drive plant adaptation to arid environments. By integrating metabolomics, systems biology, and functional genomics, our research bridges fundamental biochemistry with sustainable agricultural solutions. Our work focuses on two primary pillars: Desert Viticulture, where we explore how extreme heat and water stress shape the chemical complexity of grapes and wine; and Seed Metabolism, where we profile the regulatory networks governing development, germination, and nutritional quality. Using the Negev Desert as a natural laboratory, we aim to develop climate-resilient crops and enhance food security in the face of global environmental change.
