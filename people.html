Graduate Students

Kidanemaryam Reta

Dr. Kidanemaryam Reta is a Postdoctoral Researcher in the Fait Lab at the French Associates Institute for Agriculture and Biotechnology of Drylands (BGU). His research focuses on plant metabolomics, where he integrates metabolic profiling with plant physiology to understand crop resilience in arid environments. With a background in plant breeding and genetic improvement, Dr. Reta currently utilizes mass spectrometry-based metabolomics to identify biomarkers for drought and salinity tolerance in crops such as sorghum, wheat, and grapevine. His work aims to decode the metabolic signatures of "climate-smart" germplasm to support sustainable agriculture in moisture-stressed regions. .

Former Students

Asfaw Degu

Dr. Asfaw Degu is a plant scientist and biochemist specializing in crop resilience and horticultural quality. His research utilizes transcriptomics and metabolite profiling to understand how plants specifically Arabica coffee and grapevines adapt to environmental stressors like drought. With a PhD and postdoctoral experience from the Agricultural Research Organization (ARO) in Israel, Dr. Degu focuses on identifying the genetic and biochemical markers necessary to develop climate-resilient agriculture. His work also extends to improving seed germination and post-harvest quality in ornamental crops.

Noam Reshef

Dr. Noam Reshef is a Principal Investigator at the Volcani Institute (ARO), leading the Table Grape Breeding Program. His research integrates metabolomics and transcriptomics to understand how environmental factors and genetics regulate fruit quality, specifically flavor and organic acid composition. Previously a postdoctoral fellow at Cornell University (VitisGen2 project), he holds a Ph.D. from Ben-Gurion University, where he studied the metabolic responses of wine grapes to desert conditions. His work aims to develop high-quality, resilient grape cultivars through advanced physiological and molecular breeding.

Uri Hocberg

Dr. Uri Hochberg is a Principal Investigator at the Volcani Institute (ARO), specializing in plant hydraulics and drought physiology. His research focuses on the mechanisms of water transport, stomatal regulation, and xylem function, particularly in grapevines. By combining physiological monitoring with structural analysis, his lab develops sustainable irrigation strategies to improve crop resilience in arid environments. Dr. Hochberg earned his Ph.D. from Ben-Gurion University and conducted postdoctoral research at Cornell University and the University of Clermont Ferrand.

David Toubiana

Dr. David Toubiana is a researcher specializing in computational biology and plant metabolomics. He is known for developing advanced correlation-based network analysis and machine learning tools to map metabolic pathways in crops like tomatoes and potatoes. His work focuses on integrating large-scale omics data to understand how plants respond to environmental stressors, such as drought and nitrogen deficiency. Dr. Toubiana completed his Ph.D. at Ben-Gurion University of the Negev and conducted research at the University of California, Davis, where he applied network biology to improve crop breeding and trait prediction. He is currently the CEO and Founder of BiolomiX, a company focused on molecular networks and AI in preclinical research.

Kelem Gashu Alamrie

Dr. Kelem Gashu is a plant biologist and researcher specializing in plant physiology and metabolic flux analysis. His research focuses on the intersection of photorespiration and one-carbon (1C) metabolism, specifically how plants regulate metabolic networks under environmental stressors like heat and topo-climate variation. Currently a postdoctoral researcher at Michigan State University, Dr. Gashu utilizes advanced isotopic labeling and high-resolution mass spectrometry to quantify carbon flux and metabolic responses in species such as Arabidopsis and grapevines (Vitis vinifera). Dr. Gashu earned his Ph.D. from Ben-Gurion University of the Negev, where he studied the metabolism of berries in response to temerature variations.

Pankaj Verma

Dr. Pankaj Kumar Verma is a plant molecular biologist and postdoctoral researcher specializing in abiotic stress and plant metabolomics. His research focuses on the molecular mechanisms of heat and salt tolerance in crops, particularly wine grapes, using a combination of metagenomics and high-resolution mass spectrometry. Dr. Verma's work aims to identify metabolic biomarkers and genetic pathways that can be targeted to develop climate resilient agriculture. He earned his Ph.D. from the National Botanical Research Institute (NBRI) in India, where he investigated arsenic stress tolerance in rice, and has since conducted extensive research at the Jacob Blaustein Institutes for Desert Research at Ben Gurion University.
